Diamond Mine "Mir", Yakutia

RECOMMENDED

The most popular choice of our clients.

The man-made diamond quarry was called "Mir", Nowadays, the mine is no longer operating, and tourists have a chance to look at a colossal quarry - one and a half kilometers in diameter! The Yakuts believe that diamond deposits in their lands are associated with intervention from above: once it was so cold that a deity hovering in the sky froze his hand and dropped a whole bag of diamonds on the ground.

Krenitsyn volcano, Kuril Islands

A quick start in exploring the nature of the Far East.

Located on the Onekotan Island, the active volcano Krenitsyn is a truly unique phenomenon. This is not just a volcano, but a volcano within a volcano. Around its top, overgrown with forests, a ring of lake closes, enclosed in flowering slopes - calderas. The mesmerizing landscape is complemented by the haze enveloping the lake - neither give nor take the scenery for "Jurassic Park"!

Lake Jack London, Magadan

STAR NIGHT

Ideal for large groups of people.

A huge ten-kilometer reservoir with four picturesque islets owes its name to an unusual find: an expedition that discovered the lake found on its shore a book by the famous writer Jack London. How the book got here and who the mysterious intellectual turned out to be in this deserted area of the Magadan region is unknown to this day.

Glass Bay, Vladivostok

Awesome location to visit!

The pebbles and sand of the bay mixed with glass cut by the sea. Red, blue, yellow, green - what kind of pebbles are not here! This is an ideal place for a spectacular photo shoot: glass pebbles shimmer in the sun with all the colors of the rainbow, sparkle and throw motley glare everywhere. Glass Bay is a unique phenomenon; you will not find a similar place in our country anymore.

Oymyakon, Yakutia

WINTER SEASON

For the most persistent people.

Oymyakon is a place where winter lasts almost 9 months, eyelashes become covered with frost in a couple of minutes in the cold. The translation of the name of this picturesque corner of Yakutia, at first glance, is completely illogical: "non-freezing water", but ironically, precisely because of the abnormally low temperatures, the soil and underground rivers freeze so much that their volume pushes the non-frozen water out. No wonder Oymyakon bears the proud status of the coldest city in Russia!

Kutkhiny Bati, Kamchatka

You probably haven't seen anything like this before.

Pumice rocks, sacred for the Itelmens, are a real place of power. According to legend, the creator of the world, Kutkh, who loved to fish on the Kuril Lake, folded his boats upside down so that he could go fishing at any time. This is how the Kutkhins Baty appeared - in translation "boats Kutkha" - picturesque slopes, the views of which are now admired by tourists. Locals make Kutkh figurines from wood and bone: they are purchased not only as souvenirs, but also as talismans.
